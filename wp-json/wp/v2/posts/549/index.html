Looker studio is an open source tool by Google to create your dashboards and communicate your findings via interactive visualizations. Even though it has a wide range of visualization options to choose from, there are still times when we want to present data in a unique way or want an additional feature in the available charts. After what seems to be an entire era of relying on Looker Studio\u2019s available charts to display our work, we can finally make our own Custom Visualizations.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The new Custom Visualization Feature<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

This upcoming feature in looker studio is in private beta and will allow users to create their desired visualization, embed it into their reports and share them with others! The only drawback that I consider here is that to create these visualizations, you need to have at least beginner level knowledge of coding languages like HTML, CSS and javascript. You can also use javascript libraries in your visualizations (For example; d3.js<\/span><\/a>) but the source needs to be in the main source of the visualization. External references to the libraries will not work.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A guide to make and use a custom visualization is available in this codelab<\/span><\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

These Custom Visualizations are a one-time investment. You can make one and reuse it as many times as you want in different looker studio reports. Awesome, right? But I am not done yet. You can also publish your custom visualizations to the Looker Studio Community by following these steps<\/span><\/a>. A few featured visualizations by The Looker Studio Community can be found here<\/span><\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What we did<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

We at MarketLytics, love to experiment and couldn\u2019t hold back when we heard of this awesome feature being introduced by Looker Studio. Here are some of our favorite custom-built visualizations in d3.js<\/span><\/a> for the Looker Studio Community!<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Conversion Funnel<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Link: <\/strong>gs:\/\/lookerstudio-visualization\/conversion-funnel<\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n

One of the most important insights for decision makers is to know the performance of the funnel that leads to user conversion. In addition to that, they also need to know what are the pain points of their conversion funnel where users drop. For Example, if the checkout page on your ecommerce site takes a lot of time to load, or the available payment options are inconvenient and the user leaves before making a purchase.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Google Analytics offer such insights via it\u2019s Goal Flow<\/span><\/a> and Funnel Visualization<\/span><\/a> report. We thought it\u2019d be useful to have a graphical representation of a Conversion Funnel in Looker Studio.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This conversion funnel represents each step of your goal as a bar. And the shaded area above each bar is the drop off rate of the user from the previous step. You can also get the respective numbers by hovering over the bars. Try it out here<\/span><\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n
