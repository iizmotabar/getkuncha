1. G<\/span>etting your hands dirty with UTM parameters<\/strong><\/h3>\n

UTM(Urchin Tracking Module) code is a simple code that is attached to the actual URL to track visitor information. <\/span>UTM parameters come handy for email tracking in google analytics. There are five UTM components that can be added to the URL.<\/span><\/p>\n

utm_source* : <\/strong>Identify the source of traffic i.e google, bing, stackoverflow<\/span><\/p>\n

utm_medium* : <\/strong>Identify the medium i.e email<\/span><\/p>\n

utm_campaign* : <\/strong>Identify the specific campaign strategy i.e winter_sale <\/span><\/p>\n

utm_content : <\/strong>used for A\/B testing<\/span><\/p>\n

utm_term: <\/strong>used for paid search to track keyword<\/span><\/p>\n

The first three parameters are required for tracking any campaign\/URL and the rest are optional. These UTM tags are added at the end of URL by separating with a question mark(?).<\/span><\/p>\n

2. <\/span>Embedding UTM parameters in URL<\/strong><\/h3>\n

There are two common ways to embed UTM tags in a URL. <\/span><\/p>\n

1. Adding tags by yourself<\/strong><\/p>\n

Only follow this method if you have practiced enough with UTM <\/span>tags otherwise you will end up with zero results. Let\u2019s say you are tracking a newsletter email campaign and the actual campaign URL is <\/span>https:\/\/campaigntracking.com<\/strong><\/p>\n

Let\u2019s add the UTM tags to the above URL.<\/span><\/p>\n

First, add a question mark to separate the url from the tags<\/span><\/p>\n

https:\/\/campaigntracking.com?<\/strong><\/p>\n

Next, enter utm_source tag<\/span><\/p>\n

https:\/\/campaigntracking.com?utm_source=newsletter<\/strong><\/p>\n

To separate the UTM tags, we will use the \u2018&\u2019 symbol. Our final<\/span> URL will be<\/p>\n

https:\/\/campaigntracking.com?<\/strong>utm_source<\/strong>=newsletter<\/strong>&<\/strong>utm_medium<\/strong>=email<\/strong>&<\/strong>utm_campaign<\/strong>=newsletter_traffic<\/strong><\/p>\n

There aren\u2019t  any defined rules to put the UTM tags, so you can insert them randomly. <\/span> <\/span><\/p>\n

1. Google Campaign URL Builder<\/a><\/p>\n

in the required fields and it will automatically generate the URL which you can promote via email. <\/span><\/p>\n

2. UTM.io<\/a><\/p>\n

 <\/span>This is another great URL builder which does not just add UTM parameters but also shortens the URLs while retaining the UTM tags. You can also use this tool for simple URL shorten purpose.  <\/span><\/p>\n

3. <\/span>Viewing email reports in google analytics<\/strong><\/h3>\n

Once you are done with embedding UTM tags and running email <\/span>campaigns, the final step is to view email reports in google analytics. To <\/span>do this, you can use two ways.<\/span><\/p>\n

1. Create an advanced segment<\/strong><\/p>\n

This is a much easier way to isolate the email traffic from the whole <\/span>data and analyze it separately. To do this, create a new advanced segment and from the traffic source column, enter the medium as email.<\/span><\/p>\n

Email Campaign metrics to measure in google analytics<\/strong><\/h2>\n

Before sending out emails, you should know the goal of your email campaigns. The goal of your email campaign can be driving more traffic to your site or generating more leads. To measure the success of your <\/span>marketing campaign<\/a> <\/span>effectiveness<\/span><\/a>, in this case, your email marketing campaigns, you need to measure few important metrics.<\/span><\/p>\n

1. <\/span>Calculate Click Through Rate for Email<\/strong><\/p>\n

Click through rate is the percentage of email recipients who clicked one or <\/span>more links in the email. <\/span><\/p>\n

In google analytics, we are going to calculate CTR for each campaign. The information you would require will be the number of emails sent and date on which the campaign was started. Follow the steps below:<\/span><\/p>\n

Step 1: <\/strong> Select the date range from the time you started the specific campaign.<\/span><\/p>\n

step 2:<\/strong> View the number of unique pageviews for each campaign (<\/span>Behavior >> Site Content>> All Pages<\/strong>)<\/span><\/p>\n

Step 2:<\/strong> Add the secondary dimension as Campaign to the email reports.<\/span><\/p>\n

filter reports<\/p><\/div>\n

Step 3:<\/strong> Apply advanced filter to view the specific email campaign.<\/span><\/p>\n

filter reports<\/p><\/div>\n

Now you can get the number of unique pageviews for the specific campaign. <\/span><\/p>\n

To calculate the CTR for this campaign apply the formula: (Number of Unique pageviews \/ Number of delivered emails) * 100. <\/span><\/p>\n

Example: (45 unique pageviews \/ 500 emails delivered) * 100 = 9% CTR<\/span><\/p>\n

The conversion rate is the percentage of email visitors who completed a <\/span>specific action on your site. <\/span><\/p>\n

For accomplishing this, you have to track the actions in your google analytics account. The actions might be a pdf download or filling a contact us form. <\/span><\/p>\n

The first step for calculating conversion rate for email would be setting up <\/span>goals for the specific actions you want to track. <\/span><\/p>\n

Next, we will calculate the number of email visitors who completed the <\/span>goals. <\/span><\/p>\n

Step 1:<\/strong> Create an advanced segment in google analytics. <\/span><\/p>\n

Step 2: <\/strong>From the traffic source column, set the Medium as email and set the condition as shown in the below image. <\/span><\/p>\n

conditional segments<\/p><\/div>\n

Note:<\/strong> Make sure to apply the filter on user level.<\/span><\/p>\n

Now you get the number of email visitors who completed a goal on your <\/span>site.<\/span><\/p>\n

Apply this formula to calculate the conversion rate:<\/span><\/p>\n

CR = (Number for users who completed goals \/ total emails sent) * 100 <\/span><\/p>\n

Example: (14 users completed goal \/ 500 emails sent ) * 100 = 2.8% CR<\/span><\/p>\n
