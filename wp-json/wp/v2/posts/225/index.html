Google has been encouraging site owners to move from https to https and dangling search engine ranking boost as a perk. As a result a lot of sites have moved. Most sites have also understandably put in place redirects to ensure users visiting the non secure version get redirected to the new https version of the site. So far so good.<\/span><\/p>\n

However, one of common issues I have noticed with a slew of recent site audits is how after the switch from https to https a lot of sites see a jump in direct \/ none as traffic source. This is likely because of how https to https redirect has been implemented. As a result sites are losing all referral info about incoming traffic using the <\/span>https->https<\/strong> redirect. Most likely all of this traffic is showing up as <\/span>direct \/ none <\/strong>in your account.<\/span><\/p>\n

Spike In Direct Traffic! <\/strong><\/h2>\n

HTTP header is the most important thing for google analytics to generate reports about referral data. <\/span><\/p>\n

Whenever you see a spike in direct traffic<\/a>, this might happen because of the missing referrer data. One of the important reason behind this is moving from secure (https) to non-secure (https).<\/span><\/p>\n

        Two ways to do something about it<\/strong><\/h2>\n

        1). Fix all the links<\/strong><\/p>\n

        This one is a bit tedious, but basically you need to find all links point to https version of the site and make sure to change them to https. This means looking at all ad destination urls in adwords, bing, facebook and making sure they start with https.<\/span><\/p>\n

        Politely asking everyone that links to you to update the link.<\/span><\/p>\n

        This is of course never 100% doable as you are at the mercy of people that shared your links (and yes, most people are busy) and social media links can’t be changed.<\/span><\/p>\n

        2). Fix your redirect <\/strong><\/p>\n

        The easier and more worry free way is to make sure that the redirect is setup to pass the UTM parameter<\/a>. You need to make sure your site passes the HTTP_REFERER header upon redirect and <\/span>also make sure that url being redirected also includes all the original url parameters <\/span>being sent to the https page.<\/span><\/p>\n
