When you start with Google Analytics it seems pretty magical but it\u2019s still not quite magic [at least not yet]. Thought I’d list of all the stuff people have asked us to do with GA and what is realistic and what is not.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

What Google Analytics can do?<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

1. <\/strong>See how many users are on your site right now<\/strong><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Real-time reports in Google Analytics is an essential feature for seeing the users who are currently browsing your site. Below are some of the reasons to keep track of real-time reports.   <\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

  1. To verify if you correctly inserted the tracking id on your site or app<\/li>
  2. See the traffic changes for a one day campaign<\/li>
  3. See the immediate effects of social share<\/li>
  4. Test changes on your site by monitoring goal completions<\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n

    2. What cities and countries your users are visiting from<\/strong><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    Google analytics is capable of showing your audience locations to see where your biggest audience exists. In order to see the location reports, in your GA reporting tab navigate to <\/span>Audience >> Geo >> Location<\/strong>.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    You can then change the primary dimension to see users by country, city, continent or by subcontinent.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    primary dimension<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    3. Finding out what devices your audience uses<\/strong><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    Google Analytics can tell you which devices are your audience using. This report is helpful to track how your site is performing on different devices. To view device reports navigate to <\/span>Audience >> Mobile >> Devices. <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    The best thing to know about device reports in GA is that you can view which brand your customers use, what type of device they are using and which operating system their devices have. You will have to change the primary dimension in your device reports.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    change primary dimension<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    4. Find your audience interests<\/strong><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    Want to know what your audience are interested in? Are they business holders or individual employers? You will find audience interest by navigating to  <\/span>Audience >> Interests >> Overview.<\/strong>  <\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    5. <\/strong>The channels that drive the most traffic <\/strong><\/h3>\n\n\n\n

    In Google Analytics you can see which channels are driving traffic to your site. Whether they are the social sites that are driving the most traffic or your site is better ranking in organic search.  <\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    You can keep track of top channels and improve your marketing strategies to get more out of them. GA has its own default channel groupings and also allow to create your own channel groupings<\/a>.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n
