If you\u2019ve a website, chances are you keep track of how many hits or sessions your website gathers over a certain period of time. But what about the journey of the user who visits your website? Wouldn\u2019t you want to know what brought a user to your turf, what sections of the website caught the user\u2019s attention and at which point did the user exit?<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Behavior Flow<\/strong> in Google Analytics<\/strong> let you know just that. They visualize the path a user follows on your website. <\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

What is Behavior Flow in Google Analytics?<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

Behavior flow visualizes the path a user follows from one page to the next or from one event to another. A path can be multiple pageviews or just a single pageview during a session. <\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Let\u2019s say a user lands on the \u201cBlog\u201d page, goes to the \u201cResources\u201d page and then to \u201cContact Us\u201d before leaving. The user path would then be <\/span>blog -> resources -> contact us<\/strong>.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Why should you look at Behavior Flow Reports?<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

Well, here are two reasons why:<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

    \n
  • The reports help you identify the most engaging content on your website. <\/span><\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • They also help identify problem areas and potential content issues.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n

    Here\u2019s what you need to get Behavior Flow Reports<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n
      \n
    1. Ample amounts of data to generate reports.<\/span><\/li>\n\n\n\n
    2. Have events and content grouping set up before they can appear in the flow reports.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n\n\n\n

      Accessing Behavior Flow Reports<\/strong><\/h2>\n\n\n\n

      To get to the reports, follow these steps:<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      In your <\/span>Reporting Tab<\/strong>, navigate to <\/span>Behavior >> Behavior Flow<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n
