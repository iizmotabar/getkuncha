Revenue attribution<\/a> would be the easiest task if a lead would just click on the ad and convert immediately afterward. In such perfect scenario, you wouldn’t have to bother yourself with revenue attribution models.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But that is not the case quite often.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What usually happens is that they visit multiple channels\/touchpoints in the journey before converting. So the real question is \u2013 how to know which marketing channels deserve the most credit?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

It depends from case to case. That\u2019s why we have various revenue attribution models so that you can calculate the value of a particular channel based on your company\u2019s campaign preferences.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There are two big groups of revenue attribution models \u2013 single-touch and multi-touch.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Single-touch models<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

We only consider one part of the journey in single-touch models \u2013 the latest or first part. In other words, these models are either first-touch or last-touch.<\/p>\n\n\n
