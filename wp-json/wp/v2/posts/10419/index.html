Google Analytics 4 is much more helpful for product managers than Universal Analytics.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Until recently, product managers primarily used Mixpanel for advanced product analytics. However, since GA4 has much better product features, parallel use of Mixpanel and GA4 should be a way to go for product managers.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Regarding Mixpanel, we have plenty of resources on our blogs, such as guides to the Mixpanel library<\/a> or activity analysis<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this article, I\u2019ll only stick to Google Analytics 4 and its product and user analytics features. In other words, I\u2019ll review the key differences between GA4 and Universal Analytics related to product management.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

So, here they are:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

  1. Higher-level measurement model<\/li>
  2. A platform for both website and mobile app<\/li>
  3. Better engagement tracking<\/li>
  4. More custom dimensions and metrics<\/li>
  5. Predictive analytics<\/li>
  6. Improved user\u2019s journey<\/li>
  7. Better visualization<\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n

    I\u2019ll review each difference in this particular order.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Higher-level measurement model<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

    Google Analytics 4 tracks events instead of sessions and page views, which was the case with the “old” version. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    You can also pick the events you find more beneficial to your business, which makes this model much more flexible.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    For example, there is a feature called “enhanced measurement.” It allows you to measure interaction with your content by enabling events you can see in the photo without any additional code changes.<\/p>\n\n\n
